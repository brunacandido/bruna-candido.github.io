# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and hiring managers in tech, geospatial science, and environmental technology sectors. They arrive from LinkedIn, GitHub, or a CV link. They spend under 90 seconds deciding whether to reach out. Context: desktop likely, but mobile happens on commutes. They're scanning for evidence of competence and distinctness, not reading prose.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Bruna Cândido, oceanographer and software developer, currently completing the Copernicus Master in Digital Earth (Erasmus Mundus) across Salzburg and Vannes. The portfolio showcases a rare hybrid profile: environmental science depth + software development practice + applied geospatial/remote sensing work. Success means a recruiter finishes the page with a clear, specific picture of who Bruna is and a reason to reach out.

## Brand Personality

Technical · Honest · Distinct

Voice: plain, specific, no marketing inflection. Shows real work. No self-promotional fluff. The work speaks; the copy annotates it. International in scope (Brazil, Austria, France) without over-explaining it.

## Anti-references

- Generic Bootstrap developer portfolio (same card grid, same blue accent, same "Hello I'm X, a developer" hero)
- Corporate / LinkedIn-printed-to-web stiffness — should show a person, not a resume
- Over-designed: no particles, no excessive gradient animations, no glass morphism used decoratively
- Anything that reads as "AI generated this portfolio template"

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't announce** — concrete project work over self-description. Dates, institutions, outcomes.
2. **Earn every element** — if removing a line wouldn't confuse a visitor, remove it.
3. **Precision over decoration** — spacing, type scale, and contrast do the visual work. Effects only where they add information or rhythm.
4. **Distinct without noise** — the profile is genuinely unusual (oceanographer + geodata + software). The design should feel like it was made for this specific person, not templated.
5. **Readable at speed** — hierarchy built for scanning: large heading, tight sub-label, body only when earned.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion respected with @media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce). Color contrast: body text ≥7:1 vs background, large text ≥4.5:1. Semantic HTML throughout.
